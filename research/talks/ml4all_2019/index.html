To represent the state of $n$ qubits, we use a complex valued vector $2^n$ elements long, called a ket.

$$|x_{n}\rangle = \begin{bmatrix} \alpha_{1}\\ \alpha_{2}\\ \alpha_{3}\\ ...\\ \alpha_{2^n}\end{bmatrix}$$

TL;DR on Quantum Computing

  • Quantum computers are not universally faster or more powerful than classical computers

  • We need orders of magnitude more qubits than we currently have for some applications

  • Current commercial hardware is a few qubits or a quantum annealer, not what is commonly considered a full "computer"
